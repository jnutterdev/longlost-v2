about

photo

I'm a writer and occasional musician living somewhere that gets cold enough in winter to make you mean about it and warm enough in spring to make you forget. I go by longlostforgotten here, mostly because it felt right and also because by the time I thought of something better the name had already stuck. I make things — songs, sentences, playlists, photographs on film I'm always a little late to have developed. Most of it is for myself. Some of it ends up here.

This site is a record. Not a portfolio, not a brand, not a place I'm trying to grow. Just somewhere to put the things I'd otherwise lose — the album I listened to on repeat for a fortnight last February, the film that rearranged something in me on a Tuesday night, the books I finished at two in the morning and couldn't stop thinking about. I've kept notebooks for years, with all the usual guilt about the gaps, and what I've learned is that a patchy record is still a record. The point was never perfect continuity.

Day to day: too much coffee, a day job I won't bore you with, a cat with strong opinions about when I should stop working. I read more books than I finish and start more songs than I record. I walk a lot, usually with no particular destination, which is also roughly how I approach most creative work.

I've been meaning to make something like this for years. The usual reasons held me back — not enough time, not enough to say, not sure it was for anyone. In the end I decided it didn't need to be ready, and that waiting for ready is just a slow way of not doing it. So here it is. Pull up a chair.
