On Keeping a Record

There's something about writing things down that makes them feel more real — or maybe more finished. Like the act of naming a thing gives it its proper shape. I've been thinking about that a lot lately, sitting with a cup of coffee going cold on the desk beside me, trying to figure out why I keep wanting to write things that no one in particular has asked for.

The short answer is probably that I'm a person who forgets. Not dramatically, not in the way that worries doctors — just ordinarily, the way everyone forgets the things that seemed unforgettable at the time. The film that wrecked me on a Tuesday night in January. The exact phrasing of something a friend said that made me laugh until my eyes watered. The walk I took last autumn when the light was doing something I'd never quite seen it do before.

A diary is not so much a record of events as a record of feelings about events — which is why it remains readable long after the events themselves have ceased to matter.

— Alan Bennett

I've kept notebooks on and off for most of my adult life, with all the attendant guilt of the gaps. There's a particular shame in opening a journal and finding the last entry is dated fourteen months ago, when you apparently had a lot of feelings about a train journey and then nothing, nothing, nothing until now. The blank pages feel accusatory.

What I've learned — slowly, and with the help of several failed attempts — is that consistency matters less than I thought. The point was never to create an unbroken record. It was to create a record at all. Even a patchy one. Even one that skips whole seasons and doubles back on itself and occasionally records the same revelation twice, as though for the first time.

What this place is for

So this site is, in part, an attempt to solve a problem I've had with notebooks: they stay on the shelf. The things in them stay private not by intention but by inertia. I write something down and then no one else ever knows I thought it, which is sometimes exactly right and sometimes a small waste.

I'm not sure yet what the right ratio is between public and private. I suspect I'll figure it out as I go. For now, the plan is to put things here when they feel worth putting somewhere — a piece of writing, a record I've been living inside, a film that changed the air in the room, a book I finished at two in the morning and couldn't stop thinking about.

That's probably enough of an introduction. The best way to know what this is will be to watch what it becomes.
