now

Last updated July 14, 2025

Here's what's going on at the moment — the usual mix of books half-finished, records on heavy rotation, and projects that keep refusing to be done.

Reading
The Dispossessed — Ursula K. Le Guin A reread. Somehow more unsettling now than when I first came to it. Keeps asking questions I don't have better answers to.
Piranesi — Susanna Clarke Only a few chapters in, already completely disoriented — in the best possible way.
Listening

Javelin by Sufjan Stevens. Have been going back to it almost every day for weeks now. It sits in a register I can't quite name — somewhere between grief and gratitude, the two things often indistinguishable from each other. The kind of record that asks you to slow down and actually be in the room.

Watching

Working through The Bear, season two. I knew it would be good going in; I didn't know it would do quite what it's doing. There are individual scenes I've thought about for days after watching them. That doesn't happen as often as it should.

Working on

A handful of demos that may or may not eventually become songs. One of them feels like it's almost there — which, in my experience, usually means I'm about to take it completely apart and start again from the beginning.

A longer piece of writing about memory and photographs that I keep abandoning and returning to. I think that's probably the right relationship to have with it for now. It'll finish when it's ready.

Lately

The days have been long and slow in a way that feels almost deliberate, like summer is asking something of me that I haven't quite worked out yet. A lot of early mornings, a lot of coffee going cold on the desk. The city has emptied out a little — the good kind of empty, where you can hear your own footsteps and the heat sits differently on everything. I've been trying to sit with things instead of rushing past them. I'm not sure how well it's going, but it feels like a worthwhile thing to attempt.
